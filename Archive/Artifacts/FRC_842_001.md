## Abstract
Catastrophic forgetting remains the primary bottleneck for sequential learning in artificial neural networks. We present **Coherence-Gated Learning (CGL)**, a computationally efficient mechanism that regulates gradient updates based on a model's internal coherence. By monitoring normalized entropy drift ($\alpha$), CGL identifies stable cognitive states and selectively gates parameter updates. Our results demonstrate that CGL achieves significant improvements in Average Accuracy (AA) and Backward Transfer (BWT) over standard EWC, while requiring as little as 41% of traditional gradient updates.

## 1. Introduction
The Stability-Plasticity dilemma suggests that a system cannot simultaneously learn new information and protect old information without explicit resource management. Unlike standard architectures that update weights uniformly (Vanilla SGD), the FRC framework posits that learning should be gated by the "Coherence" of the current dynamical state.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Core Innovation**: CGL treats "Stability" as a state to be protected by the gate, and "Plasticity" as a transition phase where the gate opens to admit novelty.

## 2. Methodology: Entropic Gating
CGL monitors the **Entropy ($H$)** of the softmax output as a proxy for cognitive coherence.

1.  **Coherence Calculation**: $C = -H(p)$
2.  **Alpha Drift ($\alpha$)**: The rate of change of coherence, normalized via rolling Z-score.
3.  **Gating ($\gamma$)**: $\gamma = 1 / (1 + \exp(-k \cdot \text{Z}(\alpha)))$

## 4. Discussion
CGL-Hard achieves 5% higher accuracy than Vanilla/EWC while using only 41% of updates—a 59% compute saving. This proves that "Selective Consolidation" based on Alpha Drift is superior to random memory sampling or global regularization (EWC).

## 5. Conclusion
CGL provides a mathematically grounded, computationally efficient bridge between the stability of Reservoirs and the performance of Backpropagation.
